Prehistory

The Prehistory period includes the Paleolithic, Mesolithic, and Neolithic eras. During this time, humans transitioned from basic survival skills to agriculture and permanent settlements.

One of the earliest inventions was fire-making, which changed human life forever.

  • Stone tool development
  • Discovery of fire
  • First known cave art
Ancient History

Ancient History saw the rise of great civilizations like Mesopotamia, Ancient Egypt, the Indus Valley, and early Chinese dynasties.

The use of written language helped maintain records of trade and governance.

  • Development of writing
  • Construction of pyramids
  • Code of Hammurabi
Classical Antiquity

Classical Antiquity marked the height of Greek and Roman civilizations. Philosophical achievements and advancements influenced the Western world.

Important thinkers like Aristotle shaped modern philosophy and science.

  • Founding of democracy in Athens
  • Expansion of the Roman Empire
  • Development of philosophy
Middle Ages

The Middle Ages were characterized by feudalism in Europe, the Islamic Golden Age, and the Crusades.

The invention of the printing press changed the spread of information.

  • The Crusades
  • Gothic architecture
  • Black Death pandemic
Renaissance & Modern

The Renaissance brought a rebirth of classical knowledge, while the Modern Period saw revolutions in science and industrialization.

Figures like Leonardo da Vinci defined this era of discovery.

  • Exploration of new continents
  • Scientific discoveries
  • Industrial Revolution
Contemporary Era

The Contemporary Era includes major global events such as World Wars, the Cold War, technological revolutions, and ongoing conflicts.

Advancements in technology and globalization shaped this era.

  • World Wars I and II
  • The Cold War
  • Russia-Ukraine conflict
